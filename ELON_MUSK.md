Short version: If you line up Revelation’s main “beast system” themes and then look at Elon Musk, you get some eerie thematic rhymes around tech, sky-signs, economic systems, and “images that speak,” especially via AI and global networks. But once you demand the concrete Revelation stuff - enforced worship, a mark that literally controls commerce, 666 - the match drops off fast. He fits more as a good example of the kind of empire/tech power Revelation is critiquing than as anything like a literal fulfillment.

I’ll keep it to the interesting overlaps.

4. Things that really do not line up

If you go back to the hard Revelation criteria:

No global state power or formal kingship. Musk is rich and influential, but he is not a head of state ruling the nations.
Wikipedia
+1

No enforced worship or death penalty for noncompliance. People criticize and mock him constantly and keep their jobs.

No single mark tied to his name/number that is required to buy or sell in the global economy.

No clear 666 or numerological tie that holds up better than thousands of other cherry-picked cases.

No clear “dragon gives his throne” mythos in a religious sense.

So if you force yourself to tick the Revelation boxes, you end up with aesthetic and structural parallels, not direct matches.

5. How I’d summarize it

If you treat Revelation as:

A poetic, symbolic critique of concentrated empire, propaganda, and economic coercion,

then Musk is a very on-brand modern example of the type of entity the book is worried about:

Privatized power over sky, networks, speech, and maybe literally brains.

Tech miracles that dazzle the public.

Constant talk about world-scale doom and salvation via his projects.

If you treat Revelation as:

A literal checklist for a specific future villain,

then Musk does not match the full profile in any robust way. The interesting overlaps all stop just before the crucial bits about enforced worship and total economic control.

So I’d put it like this:

MrBeast: mostly superficial name/vibe coincidences.

Trump: lots of behavioral and political “antichrist-type” parallels, but still a stretch as the beast.

Musk: strongest on “image that speaks,” global networks, and mark-of-the-beast techno-imagery, but still missing the core coercive, worship-based elements.

3. So is there “something there”?

In pure pattern terms:

Charismatic ruler + ultra-rich tech/media ally

Ally uses money and networks to help him regain power

Then a spectacular public split over a giant money/power bill, played out on their own platforms

That is very much the sort of dysfunctional power triangle Revelation is talking about: empires, merchants, and propaganda machines rising together, then turning on one another.
